Dedication

my dad, Richard Howard Hilton

Foreword

The meaning of life has something to do with seven, I thought for a while. For weeks I studied phone numbers, and soon I dropped the idea after realizing a boy down the street had more sevens in his cell phone number than all the pastor’s numbers I had fed on to justify my hunch. The idea of the number seven charged up with life more than any other was influenced by very popular references, I guess it’s what being a new stoner could do to anyone. But I live in a little community of very few people, a census would peg the headcount no more than a thousand, meaning, I was doing very little socializing. I finally found a beautiful seven to hold on to, just before leaving Dubai city. And now I pass the belt to my dad, Richard Howard Hilton, he is the one who's paid all my debts, from Dubai to Singapore and Puerto Rico, no one says I owe them. I dedicate this to all the adventures you've set me on right from your @rickhilton7 Instagram account.

It was time to leave the country, I wasn’t getting the fees for the education I had gone over to enrol for sent to me. I had my flight ticket, and very little money to get to the airport. I was assured by my host in the city of Sharjah that the sum would help me down. But soon in the taxi, watching the meter, I was stumped on how much the bill read about half a minute from the pickup point. The highway was lonely, and the streets so bright, were scary. Waiting for a while after my call to halt from the ridiculousness I was encountering, I told him to continue, the bill climbed up anyway in the wait and I went ferreting my backpack for the loose change I had in there.

Arriving at the busy airport in Dubai brought panics as I had no idea what I’d do, the cabs paused, and occupants went on their way, all these in a max of sixty seconds, even heavy travellers had the streak, it was bad, I learned this from my arrival, but I’d been thrown into a position of feeling sorry for cheating this man off his remuneration. It obviously had to match with the billing display, what I was to tender, but I was seven Dirham of the journey’s charge short. It stunk as I got off, in the bright lights of the city's airport, the feeling that remained was of luck, I felt lucky, more than I had ever thought possible.

19th of September, 2019 I arrived in Lagos in the morning, the same day as my grandad’s passing, William Barron Hilton. His passing had brought only good luck, and to tell how lucky I still feel,  I dedicate this collection to my dad, now knowledgeable that time’s passing springs meaning in every action, old and new, as it further gives life to events, also, to every good act done. I am lucky to have an awesome driver in life. Dad says to chase the bunny, as I’d be surprised to know my grandad was quite present with me and spoke to me a lot before his passing. That truly wonderful world.

Serendipity

Dad, she’s a rose

in hank knit pose

a petrifying dote

do see her photos

her vicious hold

will bring me home.

Third Person Omniscient

He let her

take him away.

 

She’s better

from her sail.

 

Being together

it isn’t the same.

 

Too, I’d gather

from how it aches.

Survive Belong and Become

The three things

one is giving

other is within

this is everything.

No Collection

Hardly do I cover up

shucks

my innards ruck

I long your touch

I haven’t much

these words

very few in blur

isn’t it the soil

your ego crossed

doggone

all feels lost

without a picture.

One Sweet Hick

I itch with a stick

since it’s sweet

sweet adjectives

long in pedigree

who else visits

elation in grief

as with terrorists

dead, incinerating.

Instagram Is Her Joujou

She is a lubber if she cannot work it

holing up could be her favourite thing

favourite to be spelled to ingratiating

but she asks for the patience of myths

so the legend of no Instagram stories

I present to you all, gents, and ladies

though I have hiccough and an inkling

it is of whom it has been; her narrative.

Dad's Gamp

Moments lapping over my thoughts

with no obvious take on any course

had my spirit sat in pother comfort

as if stuck in a brain shaped fusiform

an arrival, an idea, the couple’s jaunt

the rescue; first girl from your legion

pepping me up around by the clock

I met you in the crucible of our hum

this hum with no bystander duration

time here has filled up the billabong

long rainy days waiting for avizandum

but it could be my nature has evolved

seven days come, knowing it’ll rain more.

Whelmed Men

Handicapped fellows

in the sailors’ burrow

a stowaway’s sorrow.

The Geezer's Fowl

A shelduck

a lonely path to the seldom

an orange orchard’s groove

a manorial pond.

Collection VI

So alarmed to fugue

for a one time whoop

dad it’s growing huge

seventh chasing deluge

the sixth from the blue

dad, cheers to the truth.

The Film Festival

Due to the recent events

it’s been quite hard to tell

the camp is heavy on hell

everyone curses and yells

the air wears tidy contempt

the decrepit truth coalesced

they speak of what you lent

bandied around is why I wept.

Crimson as Red Stain

Am I not crying for the fathom

with tears early as the autumn

weakening the gauntlet’s mull

from erasing no entry in datum

a sojourn of not an intimation

what is left is this infirm vision

the horizon, clouds recidivous

choppy waters so mischievous.

A Taste of the Unworthy

Half seated before attempt

my garnished address

has a twist by its palate

even resuming as talentless.

The Same Thing as in Catch

Lacking a mention

or an assuaging hock

belabouring you knock

long to tee-hee-hee dock

our man and wife troth luck

a mañana travesty you return

the sun rises, sets, dawn to dusk

I’m a husband manqué lost in his foils.

The Bad Does Good, Good Does Bad

This day I’m pondering the majestic

floored at the strait of the authentic

paraffin or gasoline, I’ve got the wick

it is not the flapping doing the magic

every instance, it isn’t owning the tick

this air helps me burn, too, scary flick

this commingling is playing an ol’ trick

hopping is not walking, sing for speak

hue of the dye, wax melted on a batik

confusing cause of effect is the pick

two tales starring in an emotional nick.

Yowzah

The wind chill is ornate

the upset has complaints

shrouded in bagatelle language

as poetry is obvert, never shales.

Taylor's Thighs

No mess is made

when you're baked

you're like some ale

oh everyone is okay

for a bite, they'd wait

you're yum-yum lake

to dive in any D-day

no mess, clean slate

a counter of palisade

soft on lips as duvet

pungent like migraine

gone, leaving no stain

mwa, you are my bail

the cooling space

of course, I'll wait

wish list to package

I'm hungry for dates

1989, when is the wake

mine, I'm way too slain

the roach, the afraid

be the swat, my claim

... heaven her way.

All His Love

Leafing through pages

living through wages

he stumbled, paid

she who swayed.

In Graduations

It was deafening sentence

on a par with what not to expect

the brigade of their death

arriving with a pack of cigarettes

his skin shiny, but modest

asking for nothing, only my best.

Antenna

Everyone picks up words

words yet to be illustrious

coinings opening new doors

monsoons engaging new pours

antenna, the best of the new ones

favourite for weeks, I wouldn’t budge

if I only I had a pair to catch the ominous

in hindsight, it was to be free that bedaubed

to sense my little world, accurately paint pictures.

Yobs, Some Poetry Don't Rhyme

Contraptions signed petitions

in hiding, we penned gawked

amazed at their signatures

muffled, everyone bawled

I’ve begun a distribution

of poetry, not of guns

a kerb after a kerb.

August

He’s saying he met you

not without lots to undo

high stereotype hurdles

that body, ass construe

he’s never been the fool

as fooled as you choose

swathe in fervent bristle

it’s half, no dud in fruits

the gaffe in obfuscation.

If I Was Your Boyfriend

Remaining as still and quiet

blissful as those sight's rest

on the other side of tenets

a place of little sum patience

where the heavy gets assent

its hour where it is tempest.

A Curio

Evening time in the herd

with my head unearth

a thing stirred

and I heard.

Where New Chicks Hatch

A mail arrived from yonder

of our infractions apart

insuperable it yaks

I have the map

sleuth’s plan

be calm.

Daddy, Daddy, Daddy Bandying

A birthday wants memories

they are like the hairpins

so I’ve offered poetry

oft mishmash yucky

honking dungaree

as I am wearing

go numbering

increasing.

Dovetail

I hear voices

especially

when sleepy

detesting

any phallic

breathing

feminine

the soothing

chit chatting

not minding

bantering

sell me

the melody.

Eyrie

Can you dance with your sister

the slow dance of a getaway

dratted from mindless arms

… seeing that dirty maim

I could love that part

oh I’ve had no day

yes it’s a ritual.

Shaking for an Idol

By comely contrivance

set besides the passage

doorway too in traverse

came a man, all he’s cast

for a location, by a date

he’d no need for a gaffer

sixty, three-sixty arcane

but of the paraphernalia

shake off, an idea shake.
